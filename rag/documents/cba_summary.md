# NBA Collective Bargaining Agreement (CBA) Summary

## Overview
The NBA CBA is the agreement between the NBA (team owners) and the National Basketball Players Association (NBPA). The current CBA was ratified in 2023 and runs through the 2029-30 season.

## Key Revenue Sharing
- Basketball Related Income (BRI) is split approximately 50/50 between owners and players
- Players are guaranteed 49-51% of BRI depending on total league revenue
- The salary cap is derived from projected BRI

## Contract Rules

### Maximum Contract Length
- Free agents: 4 years with any team, 5 years with current team
- Rookie scale: 4 years (2 guaranteed + 2 team options)
- Extensions: Up to 5 years from current contract end

### Annual Raises
- Re-signing with same team: Up to 8% annual raises
- Signing with new team: Up to 5% annual raises

### Guaranteed vs Non-Guaranteed
- Most NBA contracts are fully guaranteed
- Partially guaranteed deals are negotiable
- Non-guaranteed contracts can be waived with no cap hit

## Trade Rules

### No-Trade Clauses
- Only players with 8+ years in the NBA AND 4+ years with current team can negotiate a full no-trade clause
- Players can also negotiate a limited no-trade clause (list of excluded teams)

### Trade Restrictions
- Newly signed free agents cannot be traded for 3 months (or until December 15, whichever is later)
- Players on two-way contracts cannot be traded
- Players signed using the Bi-Annual Exception cannot be traded for 60 days

### Simultaneous Trades
- Trades can involve up to 4 teams
- Salary matching applies to the aggregate of all players moving in/out for each team

## Draft Rules
- NBA Draft: 2 rounds, 30 picks each
- Lottery: Bottom 14 teams, weighted odds for top 4 picks
- Rookie scale contracts: Predetermined salaries based on draft position
- Rookie extension eligibility: After 3rd season

## Tampering
- Teams cannot negotiate with players under contract with other teams
- Tampering violations can result in fines, loss of draft picks, and voided transactions
