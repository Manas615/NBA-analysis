# NBA Trade Rules and Mechanics

## How NBA Trades Work

### Trade Deadline
- The NBA trade deadline is typically in mid-February (around the All-Star break)
- After the deadline, no trades can be made until the offseason
- Players acquired after the deadline are not eligible for the playoffs with their new team

### Trade Structure Types
1. **Player-for-Player**: Direct swap of players between teams
2. **Multi-Player**: Multiple players on each side to balance salaries
3. **Player + Pick**: Players combined with draft picks
4. **Three-Team Trade**: Involves three teams to make salaries work
5. **Sign-and-Trade**: Player signs with current team and is immediately traded

### Trade Exceptions
- When a team trades away more salary than it receives, it gets a Traded Player Exception (TPE)
- TPE equals the difference in salaries
- TPE is valid for 1 year
- Can be used to acquire a player without sending salary back

## Salary Matching in Trades

### The 125% Rule (Detailed)
For teams OVER the salary cap:
- The total incoming salary cannot exceed 125% of the total outgoing salary + $100K
- Example: Trading $10M player → can receive up to $12.6M player
- Example: Trading $20M player → can receive up to $25.1M player
- Example: Trading $30M player → can receive up to $37.6M player
- Example: Trading $40M player → can receive up to $50.1M player

### Aggregation
- When trading multiple players, their salaries are combined
- The 125% rule applies to the total, not individual players
- This allows teams to combine smaller contracts to match a larger one

## Historical Notable Trades
- 2019: Anthony Davis (Pelicans → Lakers) for Lonzo Ball, Brandon Ingram, Josh Hart + picks
- 2021: James Harden (Rockets → Nets) in a 4-team mega-trade
- 2023: Damian Lillard (Blazers → Bucks) for Jrue Holiday (to Blazers → Celtics)
- 2024: Karl-Anthony Towns (Timberwolves → Knicks) for Julius Randle + Donte DiVincenzo

## Trade Value Factors
1. Player talent and age
2. Contract length and salary
3. Team fit and need
4. Injury history
5. Draft pick compensation
6. Salary cap implications
7. Championship window alignment
